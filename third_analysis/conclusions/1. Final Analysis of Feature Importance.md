We evaluated each radiographic feature using Bland‚ÄìAltman statistics to quantify observer agreement, both between observers (Inter-observer) and within the same observer across time (Intra-observer).

- Mean Difference (Bias): Average difference between the two sets of measurements. If mean difference close to zero, there is no system bias.
- SD (Standard Deviation): If SD is small, all the differences are close to each other. If SD is large, the differences jump around a lot.
- MAD (Mean Absolute Deviation): MAD ignores the sign of the differences, focuses on how the big the difference is. Low MAD - differ little on average, High MAD - large average absolute differences - less consistent measurements

Features with lowest SD and MAD demonstrate excellent reproducibility across observers.
